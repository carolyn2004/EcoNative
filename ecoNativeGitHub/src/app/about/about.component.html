What is carbon footprint?

The carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gas emissions resulting from the manufacture, use, and end of life of a product or service. These include carbon dioxide (the most commonly emitted gas by humans) and other gases such as methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases that trap heat in the atmosphere and cause global warming. Most of a person's carbon dioxide emissions usually come from transportation, housing, and food.

3 simple ways to reduce our carbon footprint

Eliminate single-use plastic

Single-use plastics may be convenient, yet they’re fairly dreadful for the environment. Not only do they pollute our waterways and oceans, but they also require energy to produce and recycle. You can stop using things like disposable coffee cups and cutlery to reduce your company’s carbon footprint.

Use Less Water

It takes energy and resources to process water and deliver it to our homes. In addition, heating it when it is there is also quite energy intensive. Therefore, reducing consumption can improve the environment and reduce carbon dioxide emissions. Instead of taking a bath, taking a short shower, or boiling as much water as you need, try turning off the faucet when brushing your teeth.

Reduce, reuse, recycle

Whether it’s things like paper, electronic devices, packaging, or water, it all has a carbon footprint. By reducing the amount of waste you generate, reusing IT equipment, and recycling waste, you can make a real difference.
